# Mas Freight: Digital Visibility & GBP Audit Results

This document outlines the findings from our digital visibility audit across Google Search, AI/LLM visibility, and the Local Google Business Profile (GBP). 

---

## 1. Google Business Profile (GBP) Audit

Based on the screenshot provided, the current local presence for Mas Freight (Isando) has good foundational elements but requires immediate optimization to capture high-intent local search queries.

### Immediate Red Flags:
1. **"Own this business?" Link is Visible:** This indicates that the profile might be **unclaimed** or unverified. 
   - *Action:* Claim and verify this profile immediately. An unclaimed profile is a security risk (competitors can suggest malicious edits to your phone number or address).
2. **Review Deficit:** You have a 4.7-star rating, which is good, but based on only **3 Google reviews**. For B2B logistics, low review volume creates a trust bottleneck for procurement managers researching you.
   - *Action:* We need to implement a post-delivery review outreach campaign targeting highly satisfied current clients.

### Missed Opportunities:
- **No Q&A Section:** Your competitors use the Q&A section to answer common concerns before the client even visits the website.
   - *Action:* Manually seed questions and answer them. E.g., *Q: Do you transport Hazchem to Zambia? A: Yes, our entire fleet is Hazchem compliant...*
- **No Google Posts:** We cannot see any recent updates or posts. 
   - *Action:* Start treating GBP like a mini-blog. Post weekly updates featuring "Successful deliveries to Harare" or "Understanding the Beitbridge Customs RIB advantage."
- **Category:** "Logistics service" is broad. 
   - *Action:* Ensure secondary categories like "Trucking company" and "Freight forwarding service" are also selected in the backend.

## 2. LLM & Generative AI Visibility

Search engines are transitioning from traditional blue links to "AI Overviews" (like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Google's SGE). Our test queries aimed to see what these AI systems "know" about Mas Freight.

**Findings:**
- AI models correctly identify Mas Freight as a South African, family-run logistics company established in 2005, primarily focused on Zimbabwe, Zambia, and Malawi.
- AI correctly scraped that you handle general cargo, containerized cargo, hazardous goods, and bonded goods (Customs RIB).
- **The Issue:** The AI systems are heavily citing **third-party directories** (like `azfreight.com` and `shippingcontainers.co.za`) to pull this information, rather than pulling it robustly from `masfreight.co.za`. 

**The Solution:**
We need to drastically increase the keyword density and structured data (Schema markup) on the new `masfreight.co.za` site so that when an AI model is asked for recommendations, your website is the definitive primary source, not a directory.

## 3. Traditional Google Search Rankings

**Findings:**
- **Branded Search ("Mas Freight"):** You rank perfectly. If someone knows your name, they will find you.
- **Non-Branded Search ("Cross border Hazchem South Africa to Zambia"):** Mas Freight struggles to appear organically on Page 1 unless the user specifically appends your name to the query. Generic competitors dominate the top spots.

**The Solution:**
Our new website build must execute the content strategies outlined in our earlier `market-research-results.md` report. Specifically, building dedicated "Route Pages" (e.g., *Johannesburg to Harare Transport*) and "Service Pages" (*FTL Hazchem Transport*) instead of just listing these on a single generic "Services" page.
